# Getting Started with ChatGPT for Legal Research
## For Complete Beginners

---

## What is ChatGPT?

ChatGPT is a computer program (called "AI" or "artificial intelligence") that you can have conversations with. It can help explain legal concepts, find cases, and organize research.

**Think of it like:** A research assistant who's read a lot of legal materials and can help point you in the right direction.

**It is NOT:** A replacement for Westlaw, LexisNexis, or your professional judgment.

---

## The #1 Most Important Thing to Know

### âš ï¸ ChatGPT Sometimes Makes Up Fake Cases

This is the biggest danger. The AI can generate completely fake case citations that look real but don't exist. Lawyers have been sanctioned by courts for submitting these fake cases.

**The solution:** Verify every single case in Westlaw or LexisNexis before using it.

1. Go to: **chatgpt.com**

### Step 2: Get Your Instructions Ready (2 minutes)

1. On your computer, find the file called: **PASTE-INTO-CHATGPT.txt**
2. Open it
3. Keep it open - you'll need to copy from it in Step 4

### Step 3: Start a New Conversation (1 minute)

1. In ChatGPT, you should see a text box at the bottom that says "Message ChatGPT"
2. This is where you'll type

### Step 4: Paste Your Safety Instructions (1 minute)

1. Go back to **PASTE-INTO-CHATGPT.txt**
2. Select everything between the `---` lines (all the text)
3. Copy it (Ctrl+C on Windows, Cmd+C on Mac)

ChatGPT will respond saying it understands the rules. Something like:

When you see that, you're ready!

## Your First Research Question

Let's try a simple example:

**Type this into ChatGPT:**

```
I need Wisconsin state law on negligence.
What are the basic elements?
Please verify any cases you cite through web search.
```

**What should happen:**

1. ChatGPT will search the web (you'll see "Searching..." or similar)
2. It will explain the elements of negligence
3. It might cite some cases
4. It will show you how it verified those cases
5. It will remind you to verify independently

**What you do next:**

1. Look at the cases it provides
2. Go to Westlaw or LexisNexis
3. Search for those exact cases
4. Make sure they're real and say what ChatGPT claimed

## Warning Signs - When to Stop

### ğŸš¨ STOP if ChatGPT:

- Cites cases without showing how it verified them
- Says "I'm sure" or "definitely" without sources
- Gives you specific advice about what to do in your case
- Provides case details you can't find in Westlaw/LexisNexis
- Seems too confident or the answer seems too perfect

### When you see a warning sign:

1. Type: **"STOP - Did you verify that? Show me your verification steps."**
2. Check everything independently
3. If you can't verify it, don't use it

## Good Questions vs Bad Questions

### âœ… GOOD Questions:

**Example 1:**
```
I need Wisconsin state court law on the statute of limitations for legal malpractice.
What is the time limit? Please verify any statutes or cases you cite.
```
Why it's good: Specific jurisdiction, specific question, asks for verification

**Example 2:**
```
I'm researching 7th Circuit federal law on qualified immunity for excessive force.
What's the current test? Verify cases through web search.
```
Why it's good: Specific jurisdiction and court, specific doctrine, requests verification

### âŒ BAD Questions:

**Example 1:**
```
Tell me about contracts
```
Why it's bad: Too broad, no jurisdiction, no specific question

**Example 2:**
```
Should I file a motion to dismiss in my case?
```
Why it's bad: Asking for specific legal advice, not research

**Example 3:**
```
Find me cases that support my argument that [specific argument]
```
Why it's bad: Asking AI to generate cases for a predetermined conclusion

## Common Problems and Solutions

### Problem: "I can't find the case ChatGPT cited in Westlaw"

**Solution:**
1. The case might be fake (AI hallucination)
2. Tell ChatGPT: "I can't find this case in Westlaw. Please verify it exists."
3. Don't use it if you can't verify it
4. Ask ChatGPT for different cases

### Problem: "ChatGPT's answer seems too perfect"

**Solution:**
1. Be suspicious - if it seems too good to be true, it might be
2. Ask: "What's your confidence level on this? Show me your sources."
3. Verify everything extra carefully

### Problem: "I don't understand what ChatGPT is saying"

**Solution:**
1. Ask: "Can you explain that more simply?"
2. Or: "Can you give me an example?"
3. Or: "What does [specific term] mean?"

### Problem: "ChatGPT didn't ask for my jurisdiction"

**Solution:**
1. You tell it: "This is for Wisconsin state law" (or whatever jurisdiction)
2. Then ask your question again
