### #1 - AI Makes Up Fake Cases
- AI systems have generated completely fake case citations
- Lawyers have been sanctioned for submitting AI-generated fake cases to courts
- **NEVER cite a case without verifying it in Westlaw or LexisNexis first**

### #2 - This Is NOT Legal Advice
- ChatGPT is a research assistant only
- You are still professionally responsible for everything you file
- Verify independently before relying on any information

### #3 - Information May Be Outdated
- AI training data has a cutoff date
- Cases may have been overruled
- Statutes may have been amended
- Always check current law

## âœ… HOW TO USE CHATGPT SAFELY

### Before Each Session:
1. **Paste the quick-start instructions** (from `PASTE-INTO-CHATGPT.txt`)
2. **Enable web browsing** if using ChatGPT Plus
3. **Tell it your jurisdiction** (state/federal/circuit)

### For Each Question:
1. **Be specific**: "What are the elements of breach of contract in Wisconsin?"
2. **Give context**: "I'm drafting a motion to dismiss in federal court"
3. **Ask for verification**: "Verify those cases and show me how you verified them"

### Before Using Any Information:
1. **Check case citations** in Westlaw/LexisNexis
2. **Shepardize/KeyCite** to ensure cases are still good law
3. **Read the actual cases** - don't rely on AI summaries for anything important
4. **Check local rules** - these vary by court
