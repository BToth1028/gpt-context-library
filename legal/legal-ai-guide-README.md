## Quick Start (5 Minutes)

### Step 1: Read the Warnings (1 minute)
Open `legal-cheat-sheet.md` and read the "CRITICAL WARNINGS" section.

**Key takeaway**: AI makes up fake cases. You must verify everything.

### Step 2: Set Up ChatGPT (2 minutes)
1. Go to chatgpt.com and sign in (or create account)
2. Open `legal-quick-start.txt`
3. Copy everything between the `---` lines
4. Paste into a new ChatGPT conversation
5. Wait for ChatGPT to confirm it understands

### Step 3: Try It (2 minutes)
Ask a simple question with jurisdiction:
```
I need Wisconsin state law on negligence.
What are the elements? Verify any cases you cite.
```

Watch how ChatGPT responds - it should:
- Search the web
- Show verification steps
- Give confidence level
- Include disclaimer

### Step 4: Verify (Important)
Whatever cases ChatGPT provides, look them up in Westlaw/LexisNexis before using them professionally.

## Common Questions

### Q: Do I need ChatGPT Plus?
**A:** Not required, but recommended if you'll use this regularly. Web browsing helps with verification.

### Q: Can I use this for [specific legal task]?
**A:** Research and understanding concepts: Yes. Final verification: No. Everything must be independently verified.

### Q: What if I find an error?
**A:** Tell ChatGPT "STOP - that's incorrect" and ask it to verify. Then verify independently before using anything.

### Q: Can I skip verification for simple things?
**A:** No. Verify everything you'll use professionally. Simple things can still be wrong.

### Q: Is this ethical?
**A:** Yes, IF you use it as a research assistant and verify everything. Check your state bar for specific guidance.

### Q: What about client confidentiality?
**A:** Do NOT put client-identifying information into ChatGPT. Use hypotheticals and general legal questions only.

### Q: Can I share this guide?
**A:** Yes, share with any attorney who needs it. Update it as you learn more.

## Emergency Contact Info

### If You Submitted Something with Errors:
1. **Immediately** research the correct information
2. **File correction** with the court (per your duty of candor)
3. **Consult ethics counsel** about disclosure obligations
4. **Review your processes** to prevent recurrence

### If You're Uncertain:
1. **Don't file** until you're certain
2. **Verify independently** through multiple sources
3. **Consult colleagues** in that practice area
4. **Call ethics hotline** if needed

**When in doubt: Verify, ask, and delay rather than file with uncertainty.**
